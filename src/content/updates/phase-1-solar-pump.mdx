# Phase 1 Begins: Solar Pump Installation Underway

**April 10, 2025 · Operations**

Exwick Farm's first major infrastructure investment is now in motion — a solar-powered irrigation pump that will transform how we manage water across the entire property.

## Why Solar?

Zimbabwe's energy grid remains unreliable, and diesel-powered pumps carry ongoing fuel costs that erode margins. By going solar from day one, we eliminate that dependency entirely and secure a 20-year infrastructure asset that appreciates, not depreciates, with time.

The system has been engineered to supply **sufficient water pressure to irrigate the full blueberry and strawberry zones simultaneously**, with reserve capacity for the vegetable plots and livestock troughs.

## What We Installed

- **6kW solar array** — high-efficiency mono-crystalline panels, locally sourced
- **Submersible pump** — rated for the borehole depth at Exwick
- **72-hour storage tank** — 10,000L holding capacity ensures continuity through cloudy periods
- **Automated drip lines** — timed and sensor-triggered to prevent waste

## What's Next

With irrigation secured, Phase 1 moves to fencing and site preparation for the piggery rehabilitation. We expect both to be fully operational by **Q3 2025**, ahead of the first blueberry planting window.
