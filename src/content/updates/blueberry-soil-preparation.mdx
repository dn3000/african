# Blueberry Zone — Soil Preparation Complete

**March 22, 2025 · Crops**

After months of soil analysis, pH balancing, and organic matter enrichment, Exwick Farm's dedicated blueberry growing zone is ready for its first planting season.

## The Science Behind It

Blueberries are famously demanding — they thrive in acidic soil (pH 4.5–5.5) that most farm land simply doesn't provide out of the box. Our initial soil tests showed a pH of 6.8, meaning significant amendment work was required before a single bush could go in.

Over the past two seasons, we applied:

- **Elemental sulphur** to progressively acidify the soil
- **Pine bark mulch** (deep layer) for moisture retention and ongoing pH management
- **Perlite and compost blend** to improve drainage — blueberry roots are highly susceptible to waterlogging

## Zone Layout

The blueberry zone now covers **1.2 hectares** of Exwick's eastern plot, divided into three sub-rows to allow for varietal experimentation and staged harvest cycles. This staggered approach means we avoid a single peak-harvest crunch and can move product to market more fluidly.

## Varieties Selected

1. **Bluecrop** — high yield, proven export track record
2. **Duke** — early season, strong disease resistance
3. **Legacy** — premium berry size, targets high-value Canadian and EU buyers

## Revenue Projection

At full production (Year 3), this zone is projected to yield **8–12 tonnes per season**, positioned primarily for export at a wholesale price point of USD 2.80–4.00/kg depending on grade.
