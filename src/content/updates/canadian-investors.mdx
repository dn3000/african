# Welcoming Our First Investors from Canada

**February 14, 2025 · Investment**

A milestone for AfriCan: our first cohort of Canadian investors has formally joined the vision. Their commitment brings not just capital, but cross-continental expertise and market access that no seed fund alone could provide.

## Who Joined

Our founding investor cohort comprises **three individuals from the Canadian diaspora** — each with deep roots in either agriculture, commodity trading, or African development finance. Out of respect for their privacy, we are not publishing names at this stage, but a full investor prospectus is available to verified prospects upon request.

## What Their Investment Enables

The combined seed capital from this round funds:

- Full **Phase 1 infrastructure** (solar irrigation, fencing, piggery rehabilitation)
- Working capital for the **first two planting cycles**
- Partial advance on the **smart camera network** for 24/7 farm security
- Legal and compliance costs for the **AfriCan import-export entity** registration in Canada

## What Canada Brings Beyond Capital

The Canada connection is strategic, not incidental. Our investors bring:

- **Cold-chain logistics contacts** for blueberry export
- **Restaurant and grocery buyer relationships** in Ontario and BC
- Experience navigating **CFIA (Canadian Food Inspection Agency)** import requirements

## How to Join Them

We are still accepting expressions of interest from aligned investors — particularly those who see the Zimbabwe–Canada food corridor as an underserved opportunity. Minimum commitment starts at CAD 5,000 for the community tier.

Use the **Partner With Us** button on this site to register your interest and receive a tailored information pack within 72 hours.
